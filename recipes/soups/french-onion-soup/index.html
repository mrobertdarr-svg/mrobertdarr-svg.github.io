French Onion Soup

Rich onion soup simmered with wine, broth, and cider, then broiled with bread and cheese.

More Soups soup onion broiled cheese

Ingredients

  • 10 sweet onions, such as Vidalias, or a combination of sweet and red onions
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 cups white wine
  • 10 oz beef broth
  • 10 oz chicken broth
  • 8 oz apple cider, unfiltered is best
  • bouquet garni of bay leaf and parsley
  • 1 loaf country-style bread
  • kosher salt
  • ground black pepper
  • splash of Cognac, optional
  • 1 cup Fontina or Gruyere cheese, grated

Instructions

  1. Trim the ends off each onion, then slice from end to end into 1/4-inch slices.
  2. Set an electric skillet to 300 degrees F and melt the butter.
  3. Add the onions in layers, seasoning lightly with salt as you go.
  4. Cover and cook for 15 to 20 minutes.
  5. Stir occasionally until the onions are dark mahogany and reduced, about 45 minutes to 1 hour total.
  6. Add enough white wine to cover the onions and reduce it over high heat until syrupy.
  7. Add the beef broth, chicken broth, apple cider, and bouquet garni.
  8. Reduce the heat and simmer for 15 to 20 minutes.
  9. Heat the broiler and position a rack in the top third of the oven.
  10. Cut the bread into rounds to fit oven-safe soup crocks.
  11. Ladle the soup into crocks, top with bread and grated cheese, and broil for about 1 minute until melted.
