Ultimate Twice-Baked Potatoes

Loaded twice-baked potatoes with bacon, sour cream, cheddar, and green onions.

More Vegetables vegetable potato side baked

Ingredients

  • 4 large baking potatoes, cleaned and pricked with a fork
  • 8 slices bacon
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 1/2 tsp pepper
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese, divided
  • 8 green onions, sliced, divided

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F.
  2. Rub the potatoes with oil or canola oil so the skins don’t dry out.
  3. Bake the potatoes until tender, about 1 hour.
  4. Cook the bacon over medium-high heat until evenly browned, then drain, crumble, and set aside.
  5. Let the potatoes cool for 10 minutes.
  6. Cut the tops off lengthwise and remove the potato flesh, leaving enough in the skins to hold their shape.
  7. To the potato flesh add the sour cream, milk, butter, salt, pepper, 1/2 cup cheese, 1/2 the onions, and 1/2 the bacon.
  8. Beat until well blended and creamy.
  9. Spoon the mixture into the potato skins.
  10. Top each potato with the remaining cheese, green onions, and bacon.
  11. Place on a cookie sheet sprayed with nonstick cooking spray and bake for another 15 minutes.
  12. Serve hot.

Notes

  • The source notes the potatoes can also be baked in the microwave if needed.
